\opening{Dear Editors,}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled \textbf{``Upper fibers and
witness covers for the Fibonacci order-of-apparition map''} for
consideration in \emph{Research in Number Theory}.

For each index $n$, we study the birth layer $B_n$---the set of divisors of
the Fibonacci number $F_n$ whose rank of apparition equals $n$---as an
ordered subset of the divisor lattice of $F_n$. Our main contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item We show that $B_n$ is an upper set obtained by removing covering
ideals and classify its minimal generators via pairwise coprime witness
covers built from atomic prime-power birth moduli (Theorems~1.1 and~1.2).

\item We prove a primitive-versus-ladder dichotomy for atomic factors and
give complete if-and-only-if classifications for layers with
$\omega(n)\le 3$ (Section~4).

\item We establish a connected-block factorization that reduces the global
counts $A(n)=\#B_n$ and $\#\mathcal{M}_n$ to partition-lattice formulas in
terms of connected kernels (Theorem~1.3).
\end{enumerate}

These results provide a structural perspective on Fibonacci first-appearance
spectra that complements the existing counting-function approach of
Stroi\'nski and the dynamical studies of Luca--Pomerance and
FitzGibbons--Javaheri--Miller--Verga. We believe the witness-cover formalism
and the connected-block factorization are of independent combinatorial
interest and may extend to general strong divisibility sequences.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under
consideration by any other journal. Both authors have read and approved the
final version.
